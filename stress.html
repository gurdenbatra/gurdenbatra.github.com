Stress Monitor Wearable

Ideation, Prototyping, Coding

November 2016

Project to prototype a wearable to monitor and help with stress using galvanic skin responce and heart rate variability.










Circuit schematic measures skin conductance using coins/copper strips as electrodes connected to Arduino Uno. Details and inspiration of the circuit can be found here and here. Heart rate is measured using the Pulse Sensor for Arduino.

After measuring skin conductivity and heart rate variability, 5 states are computed- calm state, stress level 1, level 2, level 3 and level 4. These states are shown on the digital LED band with with 1 blue LED lighting up for calm state and respective amount of violet LEDs lighting up to represent the amount of stress. The LED flashes at the point of increase in stress before going solid as a warning notification. At stress level 4, the other side of the LED band shows nice and calming patterns to lower the user's stress. The heart rate flashes constantly on a blue LED.
